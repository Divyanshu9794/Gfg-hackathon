What is Boxing?

Boxing is a combat sport in which two opponents, typically of similar weight, engage in a contest of skill, speed, and endurance. The sport is governed by specific rules and regulations to ensure the safety of the participants and maintain fair competition.




By whom, where and when was boxing invented?

The origins of boxing are difficult to pinpoint with certainty as the sport has ancient roots and has evolved over thousands of years. Boxing-like activities can be traced back to various civilizations throughout history, with evidence of early forms of hand-to-hand combat found in ancient Sumeria, Egypt, and Greece. One of the earliest recorded instances of organized boxing comes from ancient Mesopotamia, around 3000 BCE. Sumerians practiced a form of boxing as a sport, and it was depicted in various ancient artifacts and carvings.
