What is Tennis?

The modern game is played in singles (one player versus another), doubles (teams of two) and mixed doubles (mixed-gender teams) and features differing scoring systems across international events. Tennis has a long history, but the birth of the game played today is thought to have taken place in England.




By whom, where and when was Tennis invented?

The earliest recognisable relative to tennis, as we know it, was "jeu de paume", played in 11th century France. Played in a monastery courtyard, the game used the walls and sloping roofs as part of the court and the palm of the hand to hit the ball.
