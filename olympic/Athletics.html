What is Athletics?

Athletics, also known as track and field, is a group of sports events that involve running, jumping, throwing, and walking. It is one of the oldest forms of organized sport and is an essential part of both the Olympic Games and other international and national competitions.




By whom, where and when was Athletics invented?

The ancient Greeks are often credited with laying the foundations for modern athletics. In ancient Greece, athletic competitions were an integral part of religious festivals and celebrations, with the most famous being the ancient Olympic Games. The ancient Olympics, held in Olympia, Greece, began in 776 BCE and were held every four years to honor the god Zeus. The Games featured running races, jumping events, wrestling, discus and javelin throwing, and other contests, making them an early precursor to modern track and field events.
