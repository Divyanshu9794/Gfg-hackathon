What is Badminton?

Badminton is a popular racket sport played between two or four players. The game is typically played on a rectangular court, divided by a net, and players use lightweight rackets to hit a shuttlecock (also called a birdie) back and forth over the net.




By whom, where and when was badminton invented?

The exact origins of badminton are not entirely clear, and the game has evolved over several centuries. It is believed to have originated in ancient civilizations, with a game resembling badminton being played in ancient Greece, China, and India. However, the modern version of badminton we know today can be traced back to 19th-century British India.
